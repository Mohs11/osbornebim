How does BIM work?

hopefully now you're beginning to understand what BIM is. But how does it work? Well the first thing to undersand is that there are two parts to BIM.

The Modelling side & The Information Side.

You've probably seen or a heard a lot about the modeling side of BIM but the information side is just as, and perhaps more, important. Without it, the models make no sense. BIM is changing the way we interact with information! That is where the value of BIM lies.

Below is a guide to a basic understanding

How does "Information BIM" work?

1. This is quite simple. There are a few different types of what we call "Information BIM".

2. When we say, "Information BIM", what we mean is the "i" in the BIM process, so the information.

3. Information BIM is an aspect of BIM's working enviroment that's related to the information side of BIM.

4. Each aspect is a different tool that improves the information side of how we work with the information that we capture.

5. Within BIM there are Tools that, when used can achieve specific results within a project.

6. Checklists and Tasks can be managed and stored as non-static digital data with full HD photos attached to them, making them easier to search and organise.

7. Information can be captured and uploaded using mobile devices such as tablets and phones as well as any device that has a browser.

8. Each project member is given a login in to an online enviroment called a Common Information Enviroment.

How does a "BIM Model" work?

1. The modelling side of BIM at a basic level is designing in 3D.

2. This alone creates an advantage as a 3D design can be digitally checked for errors and clashes. Other methods of checks which increase safety and efficiency are also possible including simulations of the methodology to check the process of work.

3. However combined with the Information aspect of BIM there are many more possibilities and advantages when working in 3D. Model Based Estimating involves auto-calculating the amount of materials within each element on site and assigning a cost. This speeds up and automates the estimating process.

Now that you know how it works, head over to tools to learn about the specifics.
