The Lego Simulation - 2016

A teaching simulation of the process of BIM and how it improves a project in a safe risk free enviroment using Lego.

Just like a traditional project we had a design, materials, specialists, Site Manager, Quality Assurance (QA) checklists and tasks. The Lego Simulation saw the construction of a scale Lego model of Tower Bridge completed while using BIM tools.

Our Design was stored, accessed and shared with all parties using a mobile tablet. Our materials were delivered to “site” (a meeting room) and checked using barcodes, the Site Manger would use the iPad to scan the code to identify which batch of materials had been delivered and whether they it was correct.

The Specialists had shifts and were signed in and out using the Bar Codes. We even attached a red card to one of them (don’t worry, it was all part of the plan), who thanks to the system was not allowed back on to site.

The tasks delegated what each shift was doing and all the QA checklists were completed. The entire project was completed using many of the BIM tools that will be explained to you on this page.

It was a Dom Lowry creation and was used to trial our software, introduce our people to the idea of BIM and to test how intuitive the whole process was. It turns out everyone not only found the day helpful and a great learning experience, but they also enjoyed themselves.

A short summary of BIM

At Osborne BIM means to improve our working life by using technology. By doing this we’ll be able to do our work faster and better. Using the data and modelling side of BIM we can manage our data, organise it, validate it and ensure its being used to the best possible way. BIM is here to make all our lives easier not by changing what we do, but how we do it. It affects everyone, and will bring improvements to all aspects of our business and our customers.

What is this place?

Don't Panic, this website is designed to provide you with all the information you need on BIM. It's a story and a guide to the world of BIM and what it means to us here at Osborne.

There's too much confusion and concentration on the wrong aspects of BIM, but that's ok, because we'll fix that now.

BIM Definitions - what does it mean?

Building Information Modelling

This is a term you're probably most familiar with and one that you associate modelling technologies with. At Osborne, we prefer to use the term BIM to mean Building Information Management. The modelling in BIM suggests that modelling is one of the leading parts of the process. While modelling is important in the process of BIM (regardless of which type), the value is created within the information management and organisation.It is the information management aspect which is most important.

&

Better Information Management

This is the term that we are mostly referring to when we speak about BIM. Our journey began with the information management and we have concentrated on it the most. This is the aspect of the process which bring the most value. A model is only so useful without accurate information behind it. We are dedicated to creating and using better information and managing that information in a way that brings more value to us and our client.
