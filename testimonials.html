Testimonials

Testimonials from some of our BIM users. Choose one to find out more.

John Sewell

Document Controller

Traditionally, red line drawings are received from site with details of any changes that have been made to the AFC design during the construction stage of a project. The drawings are then scanned and emailed to the project design office who then up-rev each drawing to as built status. The changes made to the red line drawings are usually hand written, can be open to interpretation and can lead to repeated requests for clarification. There is also the chance of a loss of quality during the time-consuming scanning and emailing process. By using BIM, information can be transposed directly onto a pdf file in the library, allocated for sign-off via an iPad, and then forwarded directly to the designer. This 100% digital system of data transfer is far superior than having to interpret red line drawings hand written and scanned received via email. It provides clear and concise information for the designer without any loss of quality, thus saving time and money for all parties.
