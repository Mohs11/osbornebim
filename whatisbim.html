What is BIM?

Building Information Modelling is a digital process or way of work. It is the process of building information within a model.

"Throughout a construction project of any kind, information is built or created and recorded. Traditionally, this is done on paper. BIM is a process that only changes the way we capture this information, not what we capture."
- Mohsin Hanid

BIM aims to remove the manual legwork and allow us to capture our information in an intelligent, organised and better way.

There has been a lot of talk about BIM over the past few years. Here at Osborne we've been focusing on the core part of BIM first. This relates to the digital capture of information. This is where the most value lies when considering the benefits of BIM.

What you've probably heard / How you feel now

There has been a lot of talk and activity when considering BIM within all aspects of construction over the last few years. It seems that this has mostly revolved around the modelling aspect of BIM and that most people have been caught up with the buzz words and perhaps have misunderstood what BIM actually is. It’s probably safe to say that there is a lot of confusion within the topic of BIM.

What you should know, now you're here

All you need to know about BIM on the surface is this; BIM is a process that uses technology tools, such as the digital storage of data, to improve the way we work; making our lives easier. This doesn’t always revolve around a model and most BIM processes can be used without a 3D model. The sole aim is to capture information and data in a digital way.

What does BIM mean to Osborne?

Within Osborne we have already started using some BIM technologies to our advantage and have managed to improve efficiency by doing so. There are many advantages to using BIM on our projects, some are long term and some are short term but regardless of the scale if we can bring efficiency to a project and save ourselves and our client money, we will do.

What are we doing?

Currently we are using a combination of data capture tools and 3D modelling to improve how our data is used and how it is recorded. We have a large amount of our site managers using tablets on site to capture data and instantly share it with the rest of our team. Some of our clients and subcontractors are also using our methods of work and have seen the benefits.

What are we going to do?

We aim to continue to improve the way we work by continuing to look for new ways of achieving our goals by keeping an open mind to how we work. We hope to educate our entire staff on what we are achieving using BIM involving them in the process. We will continue to use the tools we have to improve the work we do.
