Simple One Team Wessex MFF CP5 Reading 10 Car Ascot Station

Reading 10 Car | Data Capture | Daily Diaries | Task Management

Project Overview What we did Benefits Lessons

Ascot

Project Overview

Ascot is a station platform which requires lengthening. It is located on the Reading Route in to Waterloo. Using BIM Field we were able to capture data including QA Checklists, Daily Diaries, Tasks and Photos.

On site progress photo attached to daily dairy
Ascot

What we did

Allowed site users to capture their daily diaries via a mobile tablet including

  • - Automatically updating the weather in their area.
  • - The number of workers on site.
  • - A daily update including notes, hours worked on site and time stamped photos.

Used an ITP to set up the capture of QA checklists

  • - Each task within the programme that had to be completed on site had the relevant QA checklist and any drawings that need to be considered for reference.

Using Field, captured and record issues, photos, checklists and tasks.

Topcon - Total Station
Ascot

Benefits

  • - Gaining planning and accuracy efficiency by creating ITPs early.
  • - Early involvement of BIM tools and personal leads to better results.
  • - Training and project leading in BIM to support on site team.
  • - On site mobile tablets need to be issued earlier to ensure that they are used throughout the lifecycle of the project.
  • - Best BIM and data capture practices need to be cascaded down to the site teams to ensure that the data is captured in the best possible way.

Line block night works
Ascot

Lessons

  • - Successfully created and demonstrated the usefulness of creating an accurate ITP for BIM.
  • - Increased Daily Diary efficiency by auto sending them in to iGO without manual user involvement.
  • - On site data capture and workflow control.
  • - Fast and efficient collation of health and safety handover files.

Complex One Team Wessex MFF CP5 Wandsworth Town

Bridge Replacement ¦ 3D Model ¦ Data Capture ¦ Issue Tracking

Project Overview What we did Benefits Lessons

Wandsworth

Project Overview

The Wandsworth Town Project involved a bridge replacement, repairs made to a separate bridge, substation works and platform works. It was done during a 100 hour possession. Due to Storm Katie the bridge replacement was postponed.

Breaking up of the platforms at Wandsworth Town Station
Wandsworth

What we did

  • - Created our first 3D Google Sketch-up model
  • - Used the model and Navisworks to create a Simulation of the bridge replacement
  • - Created 25 new digital checklists
  • - Used an ITP to guide and define the BIM QA process
  • - Used As-Built and Design Model and the clash detection process to highlight design limitations

The Wandsworth 3D Model
Wandsworth

Benefits

  • - Successfully produced a full 3D Google Sketch-up Model for visualisation in house at minimal cost
  • - Used the Sketch-up model to highlight safety flaws in the Programme
  • - Successfully created and used an ITP to guide the QA BIM process and create an ITP template for future projects
  • - Planned to use digital QA checklists to increase efficiency and accuracy

The Wandsworth Google Sketch-up Model Simulation
Wandsworth

Lessons

  • - Using Google Sketch-up to create a 3D model is cheap and effective
  • - Using an ITP ensures a faster digital QA setup.
  • - 3D Simulations improved safety and planning
  • - Early project involvement is necessary if BIM is to be effective.

Moderate One Team Wessex MFF CP5 Portsmouth Harbour Station

Structural Repairs ¦ Painting ¦ 3D Model ¦ Data Capture ¦ Issue Tracking ¦ Daily Diaries ¦ CDE

Project Overview What we did Benefits Lessons

Portsmouth

Project Overview

Portsmouth Harbour was a large and complex level BIM project. It involved repairing and painting over 300 columns underneath Portsmouth Harbour Station. The 3D model, created by MASA Ltd, was incorrectly designed and not to specification. Because of this the high level of BIM we were hoping to achieve took longer to set up than planned.

The substructure of Portsmouth Station is blasted
Portsmouth

What we did

Allowed site users to capture their daily diaries via a mobile tablet including:

  • - Full team learning and training session with Autodesk to gain knowledge about Model Creation Process
  • - Created a Project Specific BEP which includes model development outline and specifications
  • - Used our chosen CDE BIM 360 Field and Glue to hold the model and all the data captured on site including forms and photos
  • - Manually Created just under 2000 pieces of equipment and then at a later stage mapped the equipment to the model

A photograph of night survey works
Portsmouth

Benefits

  • First BIM project using a full 3D model with element data attachment
  • Introducing BIM and the software to the site team as well as the BIM team
  • Having a platform to learn lessons on what went wrong
  • Created and debugged the overarching BEP for BIM processes when using and designing a model
  • Introduced the site team to working in a digital enviroment and created lasting and valuable relationships
  • Recorded daily diaries on site

A tablet is used on site to record information and photos
Portsmouth

Lessons

  • Learned valuable lessons when considering model set up, design, mapping process and on site BIM Field use
  • The model needs to be created and working to the specification we provide before we go on site
  • Redefined BIM usage and pushed the software to its limits by thinking of new innovative uses and problem solving work-rounds for the site team’s needs.
  • Highlighted software limitations in Field, Glue and Navisworks.
